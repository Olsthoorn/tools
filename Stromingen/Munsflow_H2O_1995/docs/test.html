Absolutely—you can “invert” it that way: generate a synthetic van Genuchten–Mualem (vG–M) dataset and fit Brooks–Corey (BC) parameters to it. That’s often the cleanest way to ensure apples-to-apples behavior in your model.

Practical recipe (robust and fast)

  1. Generate vG–M synthetic data

    • Pick (θs,θr,α,n,m=1−1/n,Ks,ℓ)(\theta_s,\theta_r,\alpha,n,m=1-1/n,K_s,\ell)(θs​,θr​,α,n,m=1−1/n,Ks​,ℓ).
    • Create a suction grid ψ\psiψ (e.g. log-spaced from ~1 cm to, say, 10⁵ cm).
    • Compute Se,VG(ψ)=[1+(αψ)n]−mS_{e,\mathrm{VG}}(\psi)=[1+(\alpha\psi)^n]^{-m}Se,VG​(ψ)=[1+(αψ)n]−m.
    • Compute θVG=θr+Se(θs−θr)\theta_{\mathrm{VG}}=\theta_r+S_e(\theta_s-\theta_r)θVG​=θr​+Se​(θs​−θr​).
    • Compute KVG(ψ)K_{\mathrm{VG}}(\psi)KVG​(ψ) via Mualem (or your chosen conductivity model).

  2. Fit BC retention first (recommended)

    • BC retention (for ψ≥ψb\psi\ge\psi_bψ≥ψb​): Se,BC=(ψb/ψ)λS_{e,\mathrm{BC}}=(\psi_b/\psi)^{\lambda}Se,BC​=(ψb​/ψ)λ; and Se=1S_e=1Se​=1 for ψ<ψb\psi<\psi_bψ<ψb​.

    • Unknowns: ψb>0\psi_b>0ψb​>0, λ>0\lambda>0λ>0. (Keep θs,θr\theta_s,\theta_rθs​,θr​ fixed to the vG values for a clean comparison.)

    • Objective (robust): minimize ∑wi [log⁡Se,VG(ψi)−log⁡Se,BC(ψi)]2\sum w_i\,[\log S_{e,\mathrm{VG}}(\psi_i)-\log S_{e,\mathrm{BC}}(\psi_i)]^2∑wi​[logSe,VG​(ψi​)−logSe,BC​(ψi​)]2 over ψ≥ψb\psi\ge\psi_bψ≥ψb​.

      • Log on SeS_eSe​ emphasizes the dry tail where differences matter.
      • Use bounds and good initials: ψb(0) ⁣= ⁣1/α,  λ(0) ⁣= ⁣n−1\psi_b^{(0)}\!=\!1/\alpha,\; \lambda^{(0)}\!=\!n-1ψb(0)​=1/α,λ(0)=n−1.

  3. Then fit conductivity

    • Choose your BC conductivity closure (Mualem-BC or Burdine-BC). Each gives a closed-form KBC(Se;λ,ℓ)K_{\mathrm{BC}}(S_e;\lambda,\ell)KBC​(Se​;λ,ℓ) (with KsK_sKs​ common).

    • Either:

      • (a) Fix ψb,λ\psi_b,\lambdaψb​,λ from retention fit, fit only ℓ\ellℓ (and optionally a small scale tweak in ψb\psi_bψb​) to log⁡K\log KlogK; or

      • **(b) Do a joint fit on θ\thetaθ and KKK with a weighted objective:

        J=∑wθ[θVG−θBC]2+∑wK[log⁡KVG−log⁡KBC]2.J=\sum w_\theta[\theta_{\mathrm{VG}}-\theta_{\mathrm{BC}}]^2+\sum w_K[\log K_{\mathrm{VG}}-\log K_{\mathrm{BC}}]^2.J=∑wθ​[θVG​−θBC​]2+∑wK​[logKVG​−logKBC​]2.

        Start from ψb=1/α,  λ=n−1,  ℓ=0.5\psi_b=1/\alpha,\;\lambda=n-1,\;\ell=0.5ψb​=1/α,λ=n−1,ℓ=0.5.

    • Fit KKK in log space and exclude/low-weight the near-saturated range (where tiny head errors create large KKK swings).

  4. Region selection / masking

    • For BC retention, only include ψ≥ψb\psi\ge\psi_bψ≥ψb​ in the objective (the dry branch).
    • For conductivity, ignore points where Se→1S_e\to 1Se​→1 (or cap weights above Se>0.95S_e>0.95Se​>0.95).

  5. Diagnostics

    • Plot Se(ψ)S_e(\psi)Se​(ψ) and K(ψ)K(\psi)K(ψ) on log scales (ψ on log axis) to verify slopes match in the dry tail.
    • Check that your fitted λ≈n−1\lambda\approx n-1λ≈n−1 and ψb≈1/α\psi_b\approx 1/\alphaψb​≈1/α; small deviations are expected because VG has a smooth “air-entry” while BC is piecewise.

Tips that prevent headaches

  • Identifiability: If you try to fit (θs,θr,ψb,λ)(\theta_s,\theta_r,\psi_b,\lambda)(θs​,θr​,ψb​,λ) all at once from synthetic vG, the problem can become sloppy. Fix θs,θr\theta_s,\theta_rθs​,θr​ to vG’s values unless you intentionally want differences.
  • Weighting: Set wKw_KwK​ so the K-fit doesn’t dominate the retention fit (e.g., normalize by the number of points and use log-space for K).
  • Initialization: The asymptotic mapping is a great start: n=λ+1n=\lambda+1n=λ+1, α≈1/ψb\alpha\approx 1/\psi_bα≈1/ψb​.
  • Purpose-driven fit: If your model is sensitive to mid-range moistures (root-zone), weight that ψ-range more.
